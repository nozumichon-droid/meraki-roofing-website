🌨️ Hail Damage Roof Repair

El Paso Hail Storms & Your Roof

El Paso's monsoon season and desert hailstorms pose a serious threat to your roof. Hail impact damage can be subtle at first—tiny dents and granule loss that homeowners often miss—but it leads to accelerated deterioration, leaks, and costly interior water damage.

Signs of Hail Damage to Check:
  • Dented or dimpled shingles
  • Cracked or split tiles
  • Damaged metal flashing (skylights, chimneys, vents)
  • Visible granule loss (bare patches, gravel in gutters)
  • Bruised or darkened underlayment
  • Spongy or soft spots in shingles

Why Hail Damage is Urgent

What starts as surface damage can quickly become a major problem. Compromised shingles allow water infiltration, leading to:

  • Roof leaks and interior water stains
  • Mold growth in attic spaces
  • Structural damage to roof decking

💨 Wind Damage Roof Repair

El Paso Windstorms & High Gusts

El Paso's desert location and open terrain make it vulnerable to extreme wind events. Gusts of 60+ mph can lift shingles, tear off tiles, and expose your roof deck to further damage. Wind damage is often progressive—initial lifting weakens the structure, allowing subsequent wind events to cause catastrophic failure.

Signs of Wind Damage to Check:
  • Missing or partially lifted shingles
  • Exposed roof decking (plywood or OSB)
  • Curled or peeling shingle edges
  • Debris impact damage (tree branches, objects)
  • Damaged or missing chimney cap
  • Torn or missing flashing
  • Loose gutters or downspouts

Why Wind Damage Requires Immediate Action

Exposed roof decking and lifted shingles create entry points for wind-driven rain, insects, and further deterioration. Left unrepaired:

  • Progressive weakening of roof structure
  • Catastrophic failure in subsequent storms
  • Water and pest infiltration

💧 Rain & Water Damage Repair

El Paso Flash Floods & Water Infiltration

El Paso experiences sudden flash flooding and heavy monsoonal downpours. When your roof's integrity is compromised—even by small leaks—water can penetrate your attic, insulation, and interior spaces. This causes mold growth, structural rot, and expensive interior damage that far exceeds the cost of roof repair.

Signs of Water Damage Inside Your Home:
  • Yellow or brown ceiling stains
  • Attic moisture, dampness, or condensation
  • Sagging drywall or ceiling panels
  • Interior water dripping during or after rain
  • Musty odors (mold/mildew growth)
  • Peeling paint or wet wall sections
  • Soft or rotting wood in attic framing

Why Water Damage is a Silent Problem

Water damage isn't always visible immediately. It progresses silently in your attic and walls, causing:

  • Toxic mold growth (health hazard)
  • Structural decay and wood rot
  • Electrical hazards and fire risk
